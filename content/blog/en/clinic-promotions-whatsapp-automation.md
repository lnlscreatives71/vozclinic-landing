## Why clinic promotions fizzle before they book anyone

A promotion has three moving parts: the send, the replies, and the booking. Front desks reliably run out of time on all three. The offer gets designed and then sits in a draft for two weeks. When it finally goes out, patients reply with real questions, does my insurance apply, can I come Saturday, how much for two teeth, and those replies pile up faster than anyone can answer them. By the time someone circles back, the patient has cooled off or booked elsewhere.

The gap is not creativity, it is follow-through. Every unanswered reply to a promotion is a patient who raised their hand and then got ignored. For a Tijuana clinic where the promotion is aimed at cross-border patients comparing two or three options, that silence is the whole ballgame.

## Send to the right patients, not everyone at once

![A patient list with only the targeted segment selected for a WhatsApp send: send to the right patients, not everyone.](/blog-assets/vozclinic-promotions-segmentation.webp)

A promotion works better when it reaches the patients it actually fits. VozClinic can target the send instead of spraying your whole list: past patients due for a follow-up, patients who asked about a specific service, new inquiries from a given city, or cross-border patients who travel for care. A whitening offer goes to the people most likely to want whitening. A cosmetic surgery consult package goes to the ones who were already asking.

Better targeting means higher response, fewer opt-outs, and a promotion that reads like a helpful nudge instead of spam. It also keeps you clean under Mexico's data protection law, the LFPDPPP, because messages go to patients who have a relationship with your clinic, on a channel they opted into.

## Timing the offer to how medical tourism actually moves

Cross-border patients book around their own calendars: long weekends, holidays, the stretch before summer when everyone wants to look their way for a trip. A promotion sent at the right moment to the right list does far more than the same offer sent whenever someone finally had a free afternoon.

Because the agent runs 24/7, the send is not limited to office hours and neither is the follow-up. An offer can go out Thursday evening and be booking Saturday consultations before your front desk is back at the desk Monday. The patient who was comparing clinics at 10 p.m. gets an answer and a slot, not a callback that comes too late.
